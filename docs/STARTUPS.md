# Blood Test Startups - Research Notes

## Companies Tracked

### 1. Function Health
- **Website**: https://functionhealth.com
- **Focus**: Comprehensive preventative health testing
- **Unique Value**: 100+ biomarkers in a single test
- **Business Model**: Membership-based ($499/year)
- **Notable**: Founded by Dr. Mark Hyman (functional medicine expert)
- **Data Sources**: TechCrunch, company website, LinkedIn

### 2. InsideTracker
- **Website**: https://insidetracker.com
- **Focus**: Personalized health analytics using blood, DNA, and fitness data
- **Unique Value**: Science-backed recommendations for optimization
- **Business Model**: One-time tests + subscription options
- **Notable**: Long-standing player (founded 2009), acquired significant funding
- **Data Sources**: Crunchbase, company press releases

### 3. Everlywell
- **Website**: https://everlywell.com
- **Focus**: At-home lab testing for various health conditions
- **Unique Value**: Wide range of tests, physician-reviewed results
- **Business Model**: Direct-to-consumer test kits
- **Notable**: Unicorn valuation ($2.9B), largest player in space
- **Data Sources**: Crunchbase, PitchBook, public filings

### 4. Thorne HealthTech
- **Website**: https://thorne.com
- **Focus**: Personalized health platform with testing + supplements
- **Unique Value**: Integrated testing and supplement recommendations
- **Business Model**: Product sales + testing services
- **Notable**: Publicly traded (NASDAQ: THRN)
- **Data Sources**: Public filings, Yahoo Finance

## Key Metrics to Track

### Financial Metrics
- Total funding raised
- Latest funding round (amount, type, date)
- Valuation (if available)
- Revenue (if public or disclosed)
- Burn rate / runway (rare to find)

### Business Metrics
- Number of users/members
- Tests completed
- Geographic presence
- Partnership deals
- Regulatory approvals (CLIA, CAP certified labs)

### Growth Indicators
- Employee count (LinkedIn)
- Job postings
- Product launches
- Market expansion
- Press coverage volume

### Competitive Positioning
- Test offerings (breadth and depth)
- Pricing strategy
- Target market (B2C, B2B, both)
- Technology differentiation
- Clinical validation

## Data Collection Strategy

### Automated Sources
1. **Crunchbase API** - Funding, valuation, employee count
2. **PitchBook** - Detailed financial metrics
3. **LinkedIn** - Employee growth trends
4. **Company websites** - Product updates, pricing
5. **News APIs** - Press coverage, announcements
6. **SEC EDGAR** - Public company filings

### Manual Research
1. Company blog posts and press releases
2. Industry reports and analysis
3. Conference presentations
4. Social media presence
5. Customer reviews and feedback
6. Competitive analysis reports

### Update Frequency
- **Daily**: News and press releases
- **Weekly**: Social media, blog posts
- **Monthly**: Funding rounds, employee counts
- **Quarterly**: Major product updates, market analysis

## Notes on Data Quality

### Challenges
- Private companies don't disclose user numbers
- Funding amounts may be approximate
- Valuation data often estimated
- Revenue figures rarely public
- Some metrics lag by months

### Verification Strategy
- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Mark confidence level for each data point
- Include "last verified" dates
- Note when data is estimated vs. confirmed
- Track data source reliability over time

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Segments
1. **Preventative/Wellness**: Function Health, InsideTracker
2. **Broad DTC Testing**: Everlywell, LetsGetChecked
3. **Specialized Testing**: Various niche players
4. **Clinical Integration**: Quest, LabCorp
5. **Technology Platforms**: Vital, health data APIs

### Trends to Watch
- Consolidation in the market
- Traditional labs going DTC
- Integration with telehealth
- Personalized medicine applications
- Regulatory changes
- Insurance coverage expansion
